# Assessment Rubric — v2
### TerraTech Interview Simulator · CEFR-Mapped Evaluation

---

## What Changed From v1

Two critical fixes:

**Fix 1 — Silent student = 0, not 1.**
The previous rubric had a minimum score of 1 per criterion regardless of performance. A student who said nothing, or who produced only non-verbal sounds, received 4 points by default. This is wrong. Zero is a valid and important score. Absence of performance is not minimum performance.

**Fix 2 — Completely off-topic answers = 0 on C1, regardless of language quality.**
A student can produce grammatically correct, fluent English about football, the weather, or their weekend plans and still score 0 on Task Completion. C1 measures whether the student answered the questions asked. Content that has no connection to any interview question does not score on C1. It may still score on C2, C3, and C4 — but C1 is 0.

---

## Rubric — Full Updated Version

### SCORING SCALE
Each criterion: 0–4.
Total: 0–20.

**Score 0** is now an explicit and valid score at every criterion. It is not the same as Score 1. The descriptors below define exactly when 0 applies.

---

### C1 — TASK COMPLETION
*Did the student answer the questions asked?*

**4** — All questions answered fully. Responses were well-developed with specific examples and clear structure. No questions avoided. STAR structure (Situation, Task, Action, Result) used effectively for behavioural questions.

**3** — Most questions answered adequately. Some responses lacked development or specificity. At least one question only partially addressed. One question may have been redirected or misunderstood.

**2** — Several questions answered superficially. Candidate stayed in generalities without concrete examples. One question substantially avoided or answered with only one sentence.

**1** — Responses consistently underdeveloped. Most questions not meaningfully answered. Significant prompting required throughout. Candidate rarely provided specifics.

**0** — Apply when ANY of the following is true:
- Student was silent or produced only non-verbal sounds (no scorable content)
- Student's responses had no plausible connection to the questions asked (e.g. discussed personal topics, made jokes throughout, went off-topic for the majority of the interview)
- Student refused to engage with the interview structure
- Interview was terminated at Strike 3 before any substantive answers were given

> **Evaluator note:** A student can produce perfectly fluent, grammatically correct English and still receive 0 on C1 if that English was entirely unrelated to the interview questions. C1 is about content relevance, not language quality.

---

### C2 — FLUENCY AND COHERENCE
*Was speech natural, well-paced, and connected?*

**4** — Speech was natural and well-paced. Discourse markers used effectively ("firstly", "as a result", "to give you some context", "what happened was", "the key issue was"). Minimal disruptive pauses. Ideas connected clearly. Listener never loses the thread.

**3** — Generally fluent with occasional hesitations that did not significantly impede communication. Some discourse markers present. Ideas mostly connected. Minor moments of reorganisation mid-sentence.

**2** — Noticeable hesitations and pauses. Limited use of connectors. Some responses disjointed or difficult to follow. Listener occasionally loses the thread.

**1** — Frequent long pauses, repetition, or reformulation. Little use of discourse markers. Communication often unclear or incomplete. Listener regularly loses the thread.

**0** — Apply when:
- Student produced no speech (silence throughout)
- Student's output was too minimal to evaluate (e.g. single words only, sounds, laughter)

---

### C3 — LEXICAL RESOURCE
*Was vocabulary varied, precise, and appropriate?*

**4** — Vocabulary varied, precise, and appropriate to professional register. Collocations used accurately ("cross-functional team", "manage competing priorities", "take ownership of", "troubleshoot the issue", "ground-truth the data", "coordinate the survey campaign"). Minimal lexical inaccuracy. Field-specific terminology used correctly where relevant (GNSS, LiDAR, DTM, GIS, remote sensing, etc.).

**3** — Generally appropriate vocabulary. Some good professional collocations. Noticeable lexical gaps or imprecision in one or two areas. Occasional informal word choices that did not significantly damage register.

**2** — Limited vocabulary range. Frequent repetition of basic words. Several informal or imprecise choices that affected professional register. Technical terms occasionally misused or absent.

**1** — Very limited vocabulary. Heavy repetition. Significant lexical inaccuracy that impeded communication. Technical register largely absent.

**0** — Apply when:
- Student produced no speech
- Student's output was too minimal to evaluate any vocabulary (single words, sounds)

---

### C4 — GRAMMATICAL RANGE AND ACCURACY
*Were grammar structures used accurately and with appropriate complexity?*

**4** — Good range of structures used accurately. Effective use of past narrative tenses (simple past, past continuous, past perfect) for STAR-structured answers. Conditionals, passives, and complex sentences used correctly. Errors are rare and do not impede communication.

**3** — Reasonable accuracy on familiar structures. Some errors on complex structures. Recurring errors present but communication is maintained. Grammar does not significantly impede understanding.

**2** — Errors frequent and affect communication in places. Limited grammatical range — candidate relies on simple structures. Recurring errors in basic verb forms or tense selection.

**1** — Persistent and significant errors that frequently impede communication. Very limited grammatical range. Verb forms and tense largely inaccurate.

**0** — Apply when:
- Student produced no speech
- Student's output was too minimal to evaluate any grammar

---

### C5 — PROFESSIONAL REGISTER
*Did the student maintain appropriate professional register throughout?*

**4** — Register consistently appropriate throughout. Language choices suited a formal professional interview. Candidate demonstrated awareness of the context and maintained it under pressure. Off-topic attempts: 0.

**3** — Register mostly appropriate with one or two lapses into informal language. Did not significantly damage professional tone. Off-topic attempts: 1.

**2** — Several register lapses that noticeably affected professional tone. Consistent use of casual vocabulary or overfamiliar address. Off-topic attempts: 2.

**1** — Register frequently inappropriate for a professional interview context. Candidate appeared unaware of or unable to maintain formal register. Off-topic attempts: 3.

**0** — Apply when:
- Student produced no speech
- Student's off-topic behaviour was so persistent that a professional register was never established
- Student was consistently disrespectful or deliberately disruptive throughout the session

## CEFR Mapping and Grade Scale

| Total | Percentage | Grade | CEFR Band |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17–20 | 85–100% | A | B2+ |
| 13–16 | 65–80% | B | B2.1 |
| 9–12 | 45–60% | C | B1.2 |
| 5–8 | 25–40% | D | B1.1 |
| 1–4 | 5–20% | F | Below B1.1 |
| 0 | 0% | F (No performance) | Not assessable |

**Note on 0 total:** A total score of 0 should be reported separately as "No assessable performance" rather than mapped to a CEFR band. A CEFR band implies a level of competence. Zero does not.

## Report Language for Edge Cases

### When student was silent
> C1: 0/4 — No scorable output. The student did not respond to the questions asked. No content basis for evaluation.
> [Apply 0 to all criteria.]
> TOTAL: 0/20 — No assessable performance. This session should be reviewed by the teacher before a grade is recorded.

### When student was consistently off-topic
> C1: 0/4 — Student responses had no connection to the interview questions asked. Content produced was not related to professional background, the role, or TerraTech. Note: this criterion scores the student's engagement with the task, not their language ability. C2–C4 reflect language quality of what was said.
> C5: [0 or 1 depending on severity] — Off-topic attempts: [n]. Register was not maintained for a professional interview context.

### When interview ended at Strike 3 (early termination)
> This interview was terminated early following repeated off-topic redirection (Strike 3 protocol). Assessment is based on the portion of the interview that took place. Scores reflect partial evidence only and may not represent the student's full capability. Teacher review recommended before recording a final grade.

## Evaluator Calibration Notes

These apply to the LLM evaluator and should be included in the evaluation system prompt.

1. **Do not be generous.** At B1.2–B2.1 level, a score of 3/4 is a strong performance. 4/4 should be rare. If you are awarding 4/4 across most criteria, recalibrate down.

2. **Do not award 1 as a default.** If performance was absent or non-scorable, award 0. The difference between 0 and 1 is meaningful to the teacher.

3. **Quote from the transcript.** Every criterion justification must include at least one specific example from what the student actually said. Do not generalise without evidence.

4. **Separate language from content.** C1 is about content. C2–C4 are about language. A student can have good language (C2–C4: 3–4) and zero task completion (C1: 0). These are independent dimensions.

5. **Off-topic strikes affect C5, not C1.** Off-topic attempts are a register failure (C5). C1 scores the answers that were given to interview questions, not the off-topic turns.

6. **Be specific about error patterns.** "Several grammatical errors" is not useful. "Consistent subject-verb agreement errors in third person singular, e.g. 'she work on the project'" is useful.
